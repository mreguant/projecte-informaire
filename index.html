The InformAire Project

The goal of this project is to build a map of pollution at the public schools in Catalonia while launching an educational campaign to raise awareness about the pollution problem in Catalonia and its harmful effects on people of all ages.

What is the data gap?

Unfortunately, pollution measurements in Catalonia are surprisingly sparse. There are few measuring stations in the territory, and many of them do not measure particles in the air, a source of pollution that is of growing concerns due to its harmful effects on children as well as long term consequences on adults, such as increased risk of Alzheimer.

 

What does science tell us about air pollution?

More and more research is telling us that airborne particles (PM10 and PM2.5) are very harmful to health. Studies show that these airborne particles can affect fetal health during pregnancy, our ability to concentrate, and increase the possibility of developing Alzheimer's or other neurological diseases, among other potential negative effects.

Why don't we have more reliable pollution measures?

There are many factors working against pollution measurement initiatives. Until recently, particle measuring equipment was expensive - only recently has it become more affordable. Using newly-developed inexpensive meters will allow us to do this from the bottom-up!

 

What good is knowing what we're breathing?

The information in these sensors will help us better understand the health risks posed to each school. This information will be useful both for the students and for all the local residents. It will allow us to design protective actions for the most affected centers and alert us during high risk episodes. It will also allow us to provide data to governmental administrations, helping them to make more informed policy decisions. Our goal is to cover the whole territory in order to have a much more accurate map of pollution in Catalonia.

And at the educational level?

We are envisioning this project also as an eminently educational initiative. We can talk with the students about the science of sensors, pollution problems and health impacts, difficulties in how to improve air pollution, data analysis, and other educational topics. The sensor installation as an initiative can help teachers create very interesting interdisciplinary projects and activities in the classroom. For those schools currently using or thinking about using bike-buses, we are certain that these meters will help evaluate the positive environmental effects of these initiatives.

Our commitment

We are committed to devoting all resources to obtaining pollution sensors. We are also committed to ensuring that these sensors are successfully installed at school or in nearby alternative locations (in local neighborhoods). Through sensors and measurements, we hope to run many educational events and outreach projects. The data will be publicly accessible to anyone who should want it. We will also actively work on creating educational materials to talk about pollution in the class. The first installations will begin in the Fall 2021 with the aim of quickly covering the region.
