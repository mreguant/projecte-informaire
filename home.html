The InformAire Project

The goal of this project is to build a map of pollution at Catalan public schools while launching an educational campaign to raise awareness about the pollution problem in Catalonia and its harmful effects on people of all ages.

What is the data gap?

Unfortunately, pollution measurements in Catalonia are surprisingly sparse. There are few measuring stations in the territory, and many of them do not measure particles in the air, a source of pollution that is of growing concerns due to its harmful effects on children as well as long term consequences on adults.

 

What does science tell us about air pollution?

More and more research is telling us that local pollution has very harmful to health. Studies show that these airborne particles (PM10, PM2.5) can affect fetal health during pregnancy, our ability to concentrate, and increase the possibility of developing Alzheimer's or other neurological diseases, among other potential negative effects.

Why don't we have more reliable pollution measures?

There are many factors working against pollution measurement initiatives, which are often not a priority. Until recently, measuring equipment was expensive. Using newly-developed inexpensive and accurate meters will allow us to do this from the bottom-up!

Sensor from airly.org

What is our plan?

We plan to deploy sensors a the entrance or playgrounds of the schools. The sensors will be in a visible area so that the students and teachers can check the status of air quality at all times. The sensors, made by airly.org, feature an LED display that convey the pollution conditions in an easy way. The data can also be accessed online in a map.

 

What good is knowing what we're breathing?

The information in these sensors will help us better understand the health risks posed to each school. This information will be useful both for the students and for all the local residents. It will allow us to design protective actions for the most affected centers and alert us during high risk episodes. It can also help governmental administrations make more informed data-driven policy decisions. The sensors also collect other important data, such as local temperature readings, that can help identify schools located in heat islands and design interventions accordingly.

And at the educational level?

We are envisioning this project also as an eminently educational initiative. Students can learn about the science of sensors, pollution problems and health impacts, difficulties in how to improve air pollution, data analysis, among other topics. The sensor installation can help teachers create interesting interdisciplinary projects and activities in the classroom.

Our commitment

We are committed to devoting all resources to obtaining pollution sensors. We are also committed to ensuring that these sensors are successfully installed at the schools. Through sensors and measurements, we plan to run many educational events and outreach projects. The data will be publicly accessible to anyone who should want it. We are also actively working on creating educational materials. The first installations will begin in the Winter 2022 with the aim of quickly covering the region.
